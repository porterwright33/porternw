About Me

Salesman. Leader. Builder.

I'm Porter Wright — a Business Management student at Brigham Young University with a background in direct sales, team leadership, and entrepreneurship. I grew up in Farmington, Utah, where I started my first business detailing cars at age 14. That early experience of finding clients, delivering a service, and earning repeat business shaped how I think about work to this day.

From 2022 to 2024, I served a full-time mission for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints across Trinidad, Tobago, and Guyana. Living in the Caribbean for two years taught me adaptability, cross-cultural communication, and how to lead and support people under real pressure. As an assistant to the mission president, I managed logistics, healthcare coordination, and personnel oversight for an entire country — responsibilities I did not expect to carry at 20 years old.

After returning home, I went straight into door-to-door pest control sales with Vantage Marketing and later Aptive Environmental. I learned quickly that the best salespeople are not pushers — they are listeners who solve real problems. That mindset helped me grow from a new rep into a pre-season team lead responsible for building and training the next wave of salespeople.

Outside of school and work, I stay active through basketball, golf, and getting into the mountains whenever possible. I read constantly — business and leadership strategy during the week, fantasy novels on the weekend. I am also genuinely excited about how artificial intelligence is reshaping industries, and I spend time exploring tools and applications that could shape my future career.

My goal is to combine my sales background, leadership experience, and business education into a career in entrepreneurship or business development — building something meaningful and leading people I care about.
